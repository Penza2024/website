Mapalo's Bio

Not as long as other autobiographies but every bit as ambitious.

Hey there, I'm Mapalo. I'm a software developer, problem solver, and someone who genuinely enjoys making things work better than they did before.

I build full-stack applications with a focus on clean code, performance, and user experience. Right now, I'm working on cross-platform event and ticketing apps using React Native, SwiftUI, and Supabase—bringing seamless experiences to mobile and beyond.

I have a thing for well-designed systems. One of my favorite books is The Design of Everyday Things by Donald Norman. I believe good design isn't just about aesthetics—it's about making complex things feel simple and intuitive.

When I'm not coding, I'm probably exploring new tech, reading about AI and automation, or diving into algorithmic trading strategies. I built an NNFX trading robot in MQL5, which taught me a lot about discipline, risk management, and the importance of testing your assumptions.

I'm also passionate about education and accessibility. I recently worked on research exploring AI-powered language translation tools to boost tourism in Zambia—because technology should help bridge gaps, not create them.

My life goal? To keep building things that matter. Maybe one day I'll write a book about software craftsmanship, or create a product that helps thousands of people solve a real problem. Who knows—maybe I'll even start a tech community in Zambia that inspires the next generation of developers.

One thing's for sure: I'll keep learning, keep building, and keep pushing myself to do better work. Because at the end of the day, that's what it's all about.

If you enjoyed reading this and want to connect, feel free to reach out. I'm always up for a good conversation about tech, design, or life in general.

Stay curious,
— Mapalo

Bio

Not as long as other autobiographies, but every bit as ambitious.

Hey there, I'm Mapalo. I'm a software developer, ICT graduate, and someone who genuinely enjoys making things work better than they did before. I'm based in Lusaka, Zambia, and I build across the full stack — from React Native mobile apps to ESP32 firmware and LoRaWAN sensor networks.

My final year project was a smart grid monitoring system for ZESCO, Zambia's national electricity utility. It collects real-time electricity data from ESP32 sensor nodes over LoRaWAN, routes it through ChirpStack, stores it in Supabase, and surfaces it across two React dashboards — one for grid operators, one for consumers. That project taught me more about the gap between a working prototype and a deployed system than anything else I've done.

I have a thing for well-designed systems. One of my favourite books is The Design of Everyday Things by Donald Norman — because good design isn't just aesthetics, it's about making complex things feel obvious. That idea shows up everywhere in how I think about software.

When I'm not coding, I'm reading about AI and automation, or thinking about algorithmic trading. I built an NNFX trading robot in MQL5, which taught me a lot about discipline, risk management, and the importance of testing your assumptions — especially when the code trades real money while you sleep.

I also care about technology as a bridge. My research on AI-powered language translation for tourism in Zambia was accepted by ZICTA in 2025 — because technology should help close gaps, not widen them.

My goal is to keep building things that matter. Maybe one day I'll write about software craftsmanship, or create a product that helps thousands of people solve a real problem, or help grow a tech community in Zambia that inspires the next generation of developers. Who knows. For now: keep learning, keep shipping.

Stay curious,
— Mapalo
